Mathematical Modelling and Computational Thinking
We analyze the fractal dimensions of healthy and diseased cells to understand their complexity using mathematical techniques. Our study focuses on neurological diseases like Alzheimer's and Parkinson's, utilizing tools such as the box counting method to quantify cellular changes and aid in early disease detection.

Understanding Cellular Behavior Through Fractal Analysis

Our project delves into the fascinating interplay between biology and mathematics, exploring how cells function under normal conditions and how they change in the presence of disease. By calculating the fractal dimension of healthy cells and comparing it with diseased cells, we uncover patterns that reveal the progression of diseases. Rooted in the hypothesis that higher stages of disease correlate with increased fractal dimensions, our research provides a unique perspective on cellular behavior and offers potential insights for medical science. Join us in this journey of discovery where biology meets mathematics to decode the complexities of life.
